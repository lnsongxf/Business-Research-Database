    Accepted by Kenneth Klassen. This paper is based on my dissertation completed at the University of Colorado. I am especially grateful for the support and guidance of my dissertation committee: David Guenther (chair), John Jacob, Robert McNown, Steve Rock, and Phil Shane. Special thanks are also due to John Phillips, Ken Klassen (editor), and two anonymous referees for many constructive comments on previous drafts. This work has also benefited from the comments and suggestions of Linda Bamber, Amy Dunbar, Michelle Hanlon, Andy Leone, Bob Lipe, Lil Mills, George Plesko, Frank Selto, Stephanie Sikes, Stan Veliotis, Martin Wu, and Sunny Yang, and seminar participants at the 2006 ATA Midyear Meeting in San Diego and the following universities: Colorado, Connecticut, Florida State, Georgia, Illinois, Kansas, Michigan State, Notre Dame, Oklahoma, Penn State, Rice, and Tennessee. I thank Thomson Financial for making forecast data available through the Institutional Brokers Estimate System (I/B/E/S), and I gratefully acknowledge funding from the Deloitte and Touche Foundation and the ATA/PricewaterhouseCoopers Outstanding Tax Dissertation Award. This paper previously circulated under the title “Book-Tax Differences, Analysts' Forecast Errors, and Stock Returns”.
