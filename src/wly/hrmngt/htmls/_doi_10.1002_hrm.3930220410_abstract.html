Employee involvement in declining organizations

Authors

  • Susan A. Mohrman,

    Search for more papers by this author

    • Susan A. Mohrman is a research scientist in the Centerfor Effective Organizations in the Graduate School of Business of the University of Southern California. She received her Ph. D. in organization behavior from Northwestern University, and has taught in the Administrative Sciences School of the Ohio State University and the Organizational Behavior Department at the University of Southern California. Dr. Mohrman's research and publications deal with the management of change, participative management techniques, union-management cooperation, and organizational design. She also has written about the methodology of long term research projects designed cooperatively by academics and host organizations. She is currently conducting a mulficompany action research project examining innovative approaches to the design of high-technology, sys tems-orien ted organizations.

  • Allan M. Mohrman Jr.

    Search for more papers by this author

    • Allan M. Mohrmn, Jr. is a research scientist at the Center for Effective Organizations in the Graduate School of Business Administration at the University of Southern California. Bqfore his present position he was an assistant professor at the Ohio State University. He earned his doctorate degree in organizational behavior at Northwestern University. He has done research and consulting on employee involvement in a number of organizations. Currently, in addition to continued a ctivity in the employee involvement area, Monty is involved with the design and implementation of computer-based office and information systems and with the design and implementation of integrated human resource systems, especially performance appraisal. Monty has published in the areas of organization development, quality of work life, performance appraisal, and computer impacts on, organizations.
