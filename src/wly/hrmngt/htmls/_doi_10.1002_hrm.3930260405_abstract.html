Proactive Human Resource Management

Authors

  • Ilan Meshoulam,

    Search for more papers by this author

    • Ilan Meshoulam is Manager of Finance and Administration at Intel Corporation, Haifa, Israel and Visiting Professor of Management, Tel Aviv University. Prior to joining Intel, Dr. Meshoulam served as Executive Vice President of Human Resources at Elscint Corporation. He has also served as a personnel executive at Digital Equipment Corporation where he was responsible for planning, systems, and administration. He has worked for Control Data Corporation as manager of international personnel planning and development and served as vice president of human resources at Elbit Computer Corporation. He researches and writes in areas of strategic human resources management and managing the personnel function. He received master's degrees in personnel management and organizational behavior from American University and Boston University, where he also received a D.B.A. in management.

  • Lloyd Baird

    • Lloyd Baird is Associate Professor of Management at the School of Management, Boston University. Dr. Baird received his Bachelor of Science degree from Utah State University, and his M.B.A. and Ph.D. in Organizational Behavior and Human Resources from Michigan State University. His articles have appeared in numerous journals, including The Academy of Management Journal, Personnel Psychology, Journal of Applied Psychology, Organizational Dynamics, and Personnel. Abstract

This article presents a model for anticipating and responding to the human resource management needs of business. It presents methods for analyzing both external fit (Human Resource Management fits the development state of the organization) and internal fit (the organization components complementing and supporting each other) of human resource management. The model and its application result from research conducted in thirty organizations ranging in size from 2000 to 300,000 employees and varing in industry from financial services to consumer products. To develop and demonstrate the application of the model, we present in-depth case studies of four organizations. Implications of the model are discussed.
