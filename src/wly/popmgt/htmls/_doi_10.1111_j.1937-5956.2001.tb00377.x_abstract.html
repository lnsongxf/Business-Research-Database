COMPARING THE ENVIRONMENTAL AND QUALITY INITIATIVES OF BALDRIGE AWARD WINNERS

Authors

  • LINDA C. ANGELL

    1. Victoria University of Wellington, School of Business and Public Management, Wellington, New Zealand
    Search for more papers by this author
    • 2

      Linda Angell joined the School of Business and Public Management of Victoria University of Wellington, New Zealand in June 2000 as a Senior Lecturer in Operations Management. Previously, Linda served on the faculty of the Management Science & Information Systems Department at the Pennsylvania State University, where she taught quality management in the undergraduate and master-level programs. Her research interests include environmental operations management, quality management practices, and work team performance. She has published in the Journal of Operations Management, International Journal of Operations and Production Management (IJOPM), Organization Studies, Production and Operations Management, and Germany's Zeitschrift fra Planung. She recently guest-edited a special issue of IJOPM focusing on environmental issues in operations management (Vol.20, No. 2, 2000). Dr. Angell holds a D.B.A. from Boston University, an M.B.A. High Distinction from Babson College, and a B.A. Summa cum Laude from the UMASS/Amherst.

Abstract

Can best-practice quality firms leverage their quality programs for environmental management? This paper explores this question by comparing the implementation of successful and unsuccessful quality and environmental initiatives in five manufacturing- and five service-sector Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award (mbnqa) winners. The results lead to a series of propositions to guide future research. While the literature suggests that quality and environmental programs are closely related, this study finds that drivers of successful environmental initiatives are not the same as those for successful quality initiatives.
